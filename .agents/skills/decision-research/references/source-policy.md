# Source and freshness policy

## Source levels

1. **Primary fact:** official manuals, release notes, regulatory filings, standards, research papers, and original datasets.
2. **Primary behavior:** direct tests, task observations, interview transcripts, product telemetry, and operational logs.
3. **Reliable secondary:** reporting or analysis with visible authorship, dates, methods, and links to underlying material.
4. **Lead only:** communities, social posts, aggregators, snippets, and unsourced summaries. Use these to find claims or primary evidence, not to support high-confidence conclusions alone.
5. **Unknown:** material with unclear authorship, collection method, date, or provenance.

Source level and truth are different. An official source is strongest for what a vendor claims or a product officially supports; it is not automatically strong evidence of adoption, usability, or business impact.

## Freshness procedure

For each decision-critical claim:

1. Identify what can expire: feature availability, version behavior, price, policy, organization, market data, or user practice.
2. Find the newest relevant primary source and record its publication or effective date.
3. Verify that the source covers the research snapshot's region, version, and population.
4. Keep older sources only when they provide historical change, method, or still-valid mechanism.
5. If no current source exists, state the search cutoff and lower confidence instead of silently using old material.

Do not use a universal age cutoff. A two-year-old standard may remain current; a two-month-old product page may already be obsolete.

## Claim support test

Before citing a source, ask:

- Does it support this exact claim or only a nearby claim?
- Is the evidence direct, indirect, or conflicting?
- Is the claim about official capability, observed behavior, or downstream outcome?
- Do multiple sources contain independent evidence, or do they repeat one upstream source?
- Does the quoted statistic preserve its denominator, sample, date, and measurement definition?

If a source only supports part of a sentence, split the sentence or weaken it.

## Triangulation

Use multiple independent sources when a claim is consequential, disputed, difficult to reproduce, or derived from a single interested party. Triangulation does not require three links by default. One direct primary source can be stronger than many derivative pages.

## Conflicts

Keep conflicts visible in the ledger. Prefer reconciling scope, date, version, and measurement before deciding one source is wrong. If the conflict remains unresolved, expose it as decision uncertainty and define a test that would resolve it.
